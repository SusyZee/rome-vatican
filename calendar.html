Facts You Need To Know About Rome


Everyone knows that the weather in Italy is just fantastic, and especially during summer, a rainfall or storm is highly unlikely. But what you should know is that summers are really hot and humid. The temperatures during the day can reach 40Â°C and August is the hottest month. It is best not to walk around during peak hours, like from 1pm to 4pm. Nights are not as hot, but extremely humid.



Unfortunately like all cities, Rome has a really bad problem with theft, especially with the tourists. Not a racist affirmation, but one has to really be aware of a certain type of ethnicity in Rome, who are real pros when it comes to robbery. Tourists must just be aware of horribly dressed people walking near you, and it is best not to keep your personal belongings in a backpack because that is their specialty, backpacks.



Not all taxi drivers are honest. Actually we can say that probably 70% are going to end up to over charge your trip, so it is best to calculate your trip from one point to another so you can get a better understanding of how long it would really take.

A brief look at other areas in Rome

Piazza Venezia

A picturesque look at Rome's ever famous Piazza Venezia. This is a very sybolic site in Rome for the Italians. The bubbles in the background are just the effect of a street artist performing his act with an audience.

Streets of Downtown Rome

A typical only pedestrian cobble stone road in downtown Rome. This is one of the streets where Rome's nightlife begins, and where all luxury shops are located which is steps away from the ever famous Piazza di Spagna.

The Pantheon

One of Rome's most famous tourist attractions, The Panntheon. Thousands of tourists per day visit this ancient monument situated right in the heart of Rome. The inside is open to the public.

Typical Homes in Rome

These are typical apartments in Trastevere, a very popular and quite an old area of Rome. These are "government projects" but in Italy, many people not in financial trouble live in these homes.

Ex Slaughter House

Also located in the historical area of Rome called Trastevere, this is the well-known by Romans, the ex Slaughter House which has been closed many years ago, but still remains a tourist attraction.

Tiber River Stairway

These are typical staircases you can find along the Tiber River. One can actually walk down and take a stroll all along the River and there are also bicycle routes, quite interesting when in Rome.

Graffiti in Rome

Like all other big and populated cities around the world, Rome has got a lot of graffitti on walls, on trains, in subway stations, under bridges and in train stations.

A Panorama of Rome

A beautiful photo taken of the west side of Rome. A picture postcard perfect with the view of the "Altare della Patria" an important monument symbol of Italy, and biggest tourist attraction.

Under the Tiber River

An example of a road and or pathway under the Tiber River where a lot of Romans do jogging, biking, or just to hang out. But unfortunately it is not advisable to be there at nightime.
