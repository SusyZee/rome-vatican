Ancient Columns in Rome

Discover very ancient columns all throught the city in Rome. These columns are specifically found all around the Theatre Marcello which is shaped like the colosseum. These marvellous columns are over one-thousand years old and are very huge and rare pieces of architecture.

Beautiful Gardens in Rome

Rome is full of amazing and colourful gardens, and this one is overlooking the Colosseum. The view is actually from one of the Colosseum's windows which has been photographed. There are amazing views from each of the Colosseum's windows where one can admire the different views of Rome.

Inside the Colosseum Arena

While in Rome, book your tour in the Colosseum. Not only can you experience amazing sights and architectures from the outside, but inside you can see the auditorium where they once held Theatre events, or duels against tigers, a myth that has been always said.

Visit the Vatican

A spectacular view of the Vatican at night with it's wonderful lights, the Vatican by night is perfect for night photos, but even a simple walk around the Vatican may be quite beautiful and relaxing. The Vatican is open to the public daily, unless otherwise stated. The best hours to visit the Vatican are the mornings which is preferrable because there is a real long line-up in the afternoon, so it is advisable the early mornings starting at 9 am.

The Vatican has thousands of daily visitors, not only in the morning, but also in the evening, since Rome may be defined as a city that never sleeps, there is a very busy night-life, and for tourists, why not visit the Vatican? It is considered much more beautiful and interesting at night so you may find as much visitors as in the morning. A visit to the Vatican is definetly a "must see" when on vacation to Rome. No one will regret this unbelievable and memorable trip to Rome and to the Vatican. A once in a lifetime trip!

There are numerous hotels, restaurants and bars near the Vatican, you just have to book a couple of months prior to your departure. The Vatican is also very near to public transportation with a variety of busses, taxis and subways to take you anywhere in Rome, although the Vatican is situated right in downtown Rome, everything, including the most popular monuments are walking distance. Rome may be visited alone by foot, or you may also rent a bike, and there are several places where you may rent bikes for good prices.
