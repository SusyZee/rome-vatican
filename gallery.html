Other Places to Visit


The Colosseum is the first tourist attraction in Rome. Anyone who comes to this city, even for a weekend or for a brief period of time will always come and vist the Colosseum. The Colosseum boasts thousands of visitors daily as it is able to hold an entire town!

Situated in the very heart of Rome is the city hall, a huge building which takes care of over 5 million habitants just in this city.

A typical view from one of the Colosseum windows and archs. As you can see, there is the view of the city roads and gardens from each of these windows and each and every one of these windows has it's own particular view depending on the numerous archs that are found all around the colosseum.

Surprisingly, there are also numerous ancient underground tunnels in the Colosseum taking you directly to other places in the city. They are not really long, since the city's core is quite small, but it is interesting enough to visit these sort of underground hideaways and see how the ancient Romans once lived.

As stated previously, downtown Rome is really full of antique columns which are very interesting, especially for photography lovers. These photos will help to create a spectacular photo album, even a portfolio but they can also become great pictures to frame and put in your home or office.

Being a country of ancient Christianity, other than the Vatican, Rome has numerous big and beautiful churches. Of course, the most popular are always in the city center, and it will take someone days to visit them all, but they are really worth seeing if one is part of this religion.

Many ancient artifacts makes Circus Maximus an open museum located just steps away from the Colosseum.

The historical center of downtown Rome is unaccessible to traffic. Cars are not allowed unless with a special permission, so it is quite useless if deciding to rent a car in Rome. It is also a waste to tour Rome by car when everything may be practically done on foot.

Other numerous ancient ruins may be found near an area called Piazza Venezia which is also walking distance from the Colosseum. The white building called "Altare della Patria" is a national Italian monument built in 1885 and completed in 1935 for the liberation of foreign invasion.
